Sales Call Script

15-minute strategy call. Use this as a guide, not a script. The goal is a signed proposal by end of week.

0:00–1:30 — Opening (don't pitch yet)

Say: "Before I tell you anything about what we do, I want to make sure this is even relevant for you. Can I ask you a couple of quick questions?"

→ "How are you currently getting new clients? What percentage comes from referrals vs. anything proactive?"

1:30–4:00 — Diagnose

If 80%+ referrals (most will be):

Say: "That's exactly where most advisors are. The referral model works — until it doesn't. If your top referral source retires or changes firms, the pipeline dries up and you can't do anything about it."

"Have you ever tried building any proactive outreach?"

If they tried and it didn't work: "Generic outreach doesn't work anymore. What's changed is that AI can now write outreach that references specific things about the person. It reads like a human wrote it — because it did, AI-assisted."

4:00–7:00 — Introduce ArgusReach

Say: "We're a done-for-you outbound system. We find your ideal prospects, write personalized emails, run the sequences, handle follow-up, and when someone replies positively we route them to your calendar. You show up to the meeting. That's it.

It goes out as you. Your name, your firm. The prospect never knows we're involved."

7:00–10:00 — Proof Points

  • Open rates: 35–55% vs. industry avg of 15–20%
  • Positive reply rate: 4–8% of contacts reached
  • Starter plan = 200 contacts/mo = 8–16 qualified conversations started
  • A SDR hire costs $65–85K/year. Our Starter is $750/month.

10:00–12:30 — Objections

See the Objection Handling doc for full responses. Most common:

  • "I've tried cold email" → execution was wrong, not the channel
  • "Too expensive" → what's one new client worth to you annually?
  • "I don't have time to manage a vendor" → you don't. One onboarding call, then show up to meetings.
  • "Compliance concerns" → you approve everything, no performance promises

12:30–15:00 — Close

Say: "Based on what you've told me, here's what I'd suggest: a 45-minute onboarding call where we build your ICP and I show you the outreach before a single email goes out. There's a $500 setup fee, then we're on a 3-month engagement.

Do you want to keep relying entirely on referrals, or do you want a second engine running in the background every month?"

→ Yes: "I'll send you the agreement and setup invoice. We can do onboarding as soon as next week."

→ Not ready: "No problem. When's a better time to follow up — end of this month or early next?"

Objection Handling

Word-for-word responses to every common objection. Read these until they're second nature.

"I've tried cold email and it didn't work."

Say: "That's the most common thing I hear — and it makes sense, because most cold email services do it wrong. They blast 10,000 people with the same email. That's not how it works anymore.

What we do is different: every message references something specific about the prospect. The channel works. The execution is what fails 90% of the time."

"I don't want to come across as spammy."

Say: "That's exactly the right concern — and it's the first thing we protect. We're not doing blast emails. Every email is personalized, professional, relevant. Volume is controlled. And every sequence is reviewed and approved by you before a single email goes out."

"I get all my business from referrals."

Say: "Great — I'm not here to fix something that isn't broken. But what happens if your top two referral sources slow down or retire? ArgusReach is a second engine. Runs in the background while you continue what you're doing. The advisors who win long-term aren't choosing between referrals and outbound — they do both."

"Too busy to manage another vendor."

Say: "You don't manage us. After one onboarding call — 45 minutes — you're done. We build everything, send everything, monitor replies, route hot leads to your calendar. The only time you hear from us between reports is when someone replied and wants to meet."

"How do I know you'll deliver?"

Say: "I can't promise a specific number. Nobody legitimate can. What I can show you is the math: 200 contacts, 4–8% reply rate = 8–16 qualified conversations started. Not all become meetings. But at 50% conversion, that's 4–8 new prospect meetings a month, consistently.

What I promise: precise targeting, quality copy, consistent execution, full transparency."

"This is too expensive."

Say: "What's your average client worth annually? [Let them answer.] So if ArgusReach books you one client per quarter — very conservative — you've returned the investment multiple times. $750/month. $2,250 for the quarter. If your average client is worth $5K/year, you needed to close one deal in 90 days to break even. That's the math."

"I need to think about it."

Say: "Of course. Can I ask what the main thing is you're working through — timing, budget, something about how it would work for your situation? [Listen.] If it's timing — when would be the right time? I'd rather schedule a follow-up now than have this fall through."

"Compliance concerns." (RIAs)

Say: "Completely valid. We never make performance promises in the outreach. Every email goes through you for approval before launch — you can edit anything. And we're not doing anything different than a human advisor making introductory calls. We're just doing it at scale, professionally."

Reply Handling SOP

What happens when a prospect replies. The monitor handles most of this automatically.

Classification

TypeDefinitionAction
Hot"Yes," "sure," "let's talk," "when are you free"Route to Calendly immediately · Notify Vito
WarmAsked a question or said "maybe"Draft answer + offer to connect
Not Now"Busy," "reach out in Q3," "remind me later"Log with follow-up date, remove from sequence
Negative"Not interested," "remove me"Polite removal auto-sent, add to DNC
EscalateLegal, aggressive, compliance risk, unclearNo auto-response · Vito notified immediately

Response Time Targets

Reply TypeNotify ClientMax Response
HotWithin 1 hourSame business day
WarmWithin 2 hoursSame business day
Negative/DNCWithin 1 hourImmediate
EscalateImmediateVito handles manually
Golden rule: A hot reply that dies because no one followed up is a failure. Own the process until the meeting is confirmed on the client's calendar.
